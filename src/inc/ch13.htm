The Ilk town of Balandri

— Chapter 13 —

When the group arrived back in Renate, Renzo stopped at the edge of the city, feet over an invisible grave in the sand. This is where the Guard from the Court of Light died. A crowd gathered at the city border, they moved toward the travelers, flowers in hand, instead of stones. They tossed the petals into the air, welcoming their friend back into their lives.

Maka pushed through the crowd, rushing over and pulling Renzo into a hug. The metalworker wept. Lupin had sent the woth ahead with a message, a story of the happenings in Irideri. Maka had begun to cry then already, happy to hear that the lonely apprentice had found peace. Ren laughed. “Not so hard!”

“Hard is all I can do! You know this.” Maka replied, speaking as curtains of tears cascaded onto the front of Ren's robes.

Lupin flashed Eka a smile. “Aaaaall thanks to Mapoleon the woth!” Eka sniggered when the woth ignored them, continuing to clean the dust off its wings. "I'm beginning to think it doesn’t want to be named.” Lupin grumbled. "Mapoleon is a great name."

Eka smirked, "woth doesn't agree."

"Ma-ma-ma-poleon?" Lupin tried, the woth did not react, it ignored the Verido and returned to its glass ball. "Not a stutterer," Lupin made a mental note of this.

Maka offered a tent to Eka, Lupin and Hush for their stay here in the city. "You are family now," Maka told them, "to me and everyone in Renate." Indeed, they stayed in town a while, and everyday a different villager would come forward to invite them to tea, to play cards at the tavern, or on excursions in the canyons. Lupin enjoyed the brief moment of stability, of feeling part of a community again. But soon, a voice whispered at their ear, calling on them to keep going.

And so, Eka and Lupin decided to depart, but first, they had to re-supply. Both went around the village to find food and other items for their trip onward.

“Was this really baked this morning?” Eka said, inspecting the loaves of bread produced by the local baker, each one more solid than the next. “This one I could use to hammer down nails."

The baker continued to knead dough, working up a sweat—perhaps they could knead rock into shapes too. “The Ilk was supposed to have arrived days ago, when it comes we’ll have better stock. In the meantime you’re going to have to make do with what’s left on these shelves. We mix in 50% pebble meal. We need to cut back on teaweet to make sure we can feed the Ilk when it arrives”.

The group had gone through most of the stores Sovereign Kurono had given them, there was little choice but to take what was available here. “If you let it rest for 24 hours with ripe bonans. The moisture will help soften the dough," the baker suggested.

"That's a good tip, thanks!" Eka said.

Another villager overheard their conversation about the Ilk. “Sometimes Ilk change their route you know, depending on the weather. I just hope it gets here soon. We’re down to bams and kappages, and even THOSE we’re running out of!”

"I thought Renate had loads of fermented kappage always?" Eka said.

"Yes, but none that we can give to transients, yield was bad this year."

Eka nodded. "Understood. Got to feed your own first."

Lupin was busy trying to find directions to the next city on their list. They already had a map but it was outdated and needed corrections. Renate housed a cartographer, working from a tent on the northern side of the camp. The dwelling was easy to find, as one of its faces bore a map of the tent city. Transients would often gather there if in need of help, all of the merchants were listed on it. It was updated regularly. Lupin visited the tent and was glad to find its occupant there, scratching away at a map that spanned the entire room. Lupin looked at it, and saw the writing atop it saying "Edonor". The map was very complex, as this was a big city, rivaling Montore. Individual houses weren't identified, but most shops were there. While looking it over, Lupin spotted Nok's workshop, tucked in between a dye artist and a hatter. This was the former workplace of Orin, Bou's mapa.

"Nok's shop!" Lupin exclaimed, the sudden sound startling the cartographer.

"Oh dear, have you been standing there long?"

"No no," Lupin said, "you're busy, I'll comeback later."

"Busy, yes. I'm always busy. Since I never stop being busy you ought to ask me what you need now, and it'll be up to me to tell you whether or not I can help."

"Oh, well my friend & I are going to Edonor. We need corrections on our map, it's a bit—um, aged. We heard there was a new village between it and Renate."

The cartographer nodded, and then waved a hand, asking for Lupin to come forward. Lupin did just that, approaching slowly, walking over the map and avoiding freshly-painted markings. The Verido unrolled the map and laid it over the floor, going down on hands and knees, mirroring the cartographer's position. "I'm Lupin."

"Mapoleon." the cartographer replied.

Lupin's eyes widened then. "Really? What a coincidence..." It was no coincidence. Earlier that morning, Lupin had passed in front of this very tent and had seen the name. What happened is that the Verido forgot, but the mass of muscle inside this head did not, it thought this combination of letters interesting and put it away for later use.

Mapoleon's eyes were stuck on Lupin's map, hand was already busy correcting mistakes, adding newly-built bridges, danger areas and such. “Didn’t happen to see an Ilk on your way over here have you? It’s late."

"An Ilk is coming here...?" Lupin was sweating now, "um... whi-which one?"

“Ilk of Balandri,” Mapoleon answered, "supposed to arrive soon, we hope.”

Lupin glanced outside through an opening in the wall, heart beating hard, trying to break out from its enclosure. After the corrections, Lupin thanked the cartographer and hurried back over to Hush. Eka wasn’t back yet and Hush was resting, fast asleep in a recess in the ground—a sure way to keep cool. Lupin waited, with eyes on the horizon. Memories of Volare rushed into Lupin's mind, memories of a place this Verido wanted to forget. Just then, Lupin heard people shouting.

“It's here! The Ilk of Balandri is here!”

The Ilk arrived from the south—not its usual path. The whole town was a swirling mess. People amassed empty carts and bags, gathering tools and extra hands, all were getting ready to help unload the Ilk. Lupin noticed a tall ramp, with people running up the steps, getting ready to greet the visitors. This ramp wasn't unfamiliar to Lupin, but these eyes had never seen it from a ground perspective. From down here, the ramp looked frail and unsteady, swaying from side to side as people scaled its numerous steps.

The Ilk arrived at last and came to stand by the platform. Ilk were impressive creatures, skinny, long-legged, giant three-toed feet, long thin faces with a great big set of horns. On their backs, they carried a small Verido town. “The Ilk of Balandri is here!!” The people of the town shouted. Not long after it arrived, caretakers were already preparing bags of teaweet grain to feed it withs. Others were busy brushing the sand of its skin, picking rocks from its toes. Other workers pushed steps over to the side of the Ilk, to make a pathway up to the town. People would be coming on and off of it all week.

"This is so exciting!” Eka said to Hush, having just returned to their camping spot in the city. Hush lifted its head to stare at the Ilk, unimpressed. Next to the hyroo, was Lupin, who bore the same disinterested gaze. Hush continued to sleep, but Lupin could not stop watching the Ilk. Eka went to pat Lupin on the head. "Aren't you excited?" But the Verido said nothing.

The quiet town from this morning had turned into a bustling open-air market. Various vendors and store owners from Balandri were setting up shop in a field, previously occupied by masses tumbling weeds. The place was full of life and colour, people laughing and shouting. Eka took a seat and watched, the market was set up right near to their spot. Already, some Balandrian Veridos were wandering the encampment. Like Lupin, they had tan-skinned, elongated ears like delicate flutes of skin, and they all bore colours in their faces, varying in placement and pattern. Balandrian were tattooed with red, rather than blue like those from Volare.

Eka was glancing from Lupin and back to the people of Balandri. “Dont you want to say hello Lew blue?”

“To what end?”

Eka took Lupin's side. "Suit yourself. They are impressive though aren't they?"

“Nearly 400 meters tall..."

"

The Voice of the Balandri Ilk was walking through town, Lupin quickly took note of it. They’d never met, but those that bear the title of Voice are easy to recognize, as they all bear a set of robes, unique to the role. Lupin had long since discarded those robes, but the blue tattoos remained. For a moment, Lupin wanted to hide, but found that no part of this body wanted to obey. The feet were cemented in sand, the torso heavy, linked to the spot by invisible chains.

Eka gave a loud whistle then. The sound caught the ear of Klev, the Voice of the Balandri Ilk. Klev's eyes turned toward them. Lupin's dumb body could not be persuaded to move, but it was too late now anyway. As the blue tattoos and ears were a dead giveaway. Klev pushed through the market folk and vendors and arrived at their little camp.

“You are far from home.” Klev said.

Lupin nodded. “Yes, though technically that would depend entirely on its whereabouts...” Only those with a birthplace with legs could say this.

“I admire you! I can’t imagine leaving Balandri. It’s very brave to venture out into the unknown like this, you must have seen much of the world."

“I have” Lupin said, while a confused soup of pride and sadness swished around in this gut. This Verido was happy to have traveled the land and to have met its people, but over time, a hard lump appeared on the side of this head. Whenever thoughts of Volare circulated in this brain, the accursed thing latched onto the memories like a thirsty vampire and gained mass. Then, the simple act of standing on one’s legs became difficult, the weight of it unbearable. “Any word of Volare?” Lupin managed to say, a hand massaging the ghostly-protrusion on this skull back down.

“Moving along the Ash plains, last I heard.”

“East?” Lupin said, alarmed. “But that would take them to the Rupture!"

Klev shared Lupin's concerns. “It’s what I’ve heard. Word is that the Ilk of Volare is dying. It’s why I felt I had to come and speak to you when I saw you were of Volare. I don't know if you know this, but their Voice died last year. Sorry to be the bearer of bad news... but it has to be known. A tragic fall. All Verido are scared, what does this mean for our race? The Ilk may walk into the Rupture, and take the city along with it. Balandri and Otora people fear that their Ilk will follow their kin in death.”

Lupin wondered what or who was causing this. "What are you doing Mago… why is this happening? Surely you would have found the words to dissuade it."

Lupin did not waste time, and went to visit Mapoleon the cartographer once more. "I need whatever update you have for the East road, Ash Plains and all the way to The Rupture." Lupin then spent a good half hour trying to convince Mapoleon to do the work. The cartographer was trying to convince the Verido not to take the Leap. "I swear to you, I'm not going there to die." Lupin said.

"I'm happy to hear it." The concerned cartographer finished adding the desired modifications.

Map in hand, Lupin returned to their encampment then. "Eka!" Lupin began, "change of plans, we're going east to intercept Vol." Eka was draped over Hush, arms laid out over its sides, caressing the large furry beast. "Okay Lew, east we go!" The Verido eyed Eka carefully, "you don't want to know why? Or is it one of those situations where you already know why and that I'm an idiot for asking. Let me guess, you spoke to the Ilk?"

Eka smirked. "You poor tortured soul."

"I'm scared Eka."

Eka had anticipated this sudden shift in mood, and was already halfway to Lupin, coming for a hug. "I know." They stood in each other arms for a long while. "If we walk we'll never make it on time." Lupin said, breaking the silence, eager, and anxious to find a solution. "I think I know how." Eka said.

Eka lead Lupin to the northermost edge of the city, where several sandfings were moored to pilings. Hush accompanied them on this trek, and bounded ahead to a carcass of a vessel, laying on it side. Its sails were in tatters, and its hull, dry and punctured. It bore the name "Tarka" Lupin walked over to it, and climbed into the cabin to assess the damage. "It's not looking too bad! But um—who does it belong to?"

"I don't know, but I know who knows." Eka said, guesturing to another ship moored in the area, a vessel with a red hull bearing the name Ethyl.

"Nono's in town!"

They returned to the site that very same night, hoping to find Nono there. Lupin could see someone was in the vessel, a light was on. This resembled that night long ago, when they'd spotted the sandfin in the dark. "Nono's home." The Verido said, relieved.

"NANA'U NONO!" Eka shouted.

They heard lots of noise inside, and then Nono's head came pushing out of one of the portlights. Nono's eyes were healthy this time, and found them in the dark with ease. "Nana'u aikana dii!" Nono shouted, smiling. "Come in! Come in!"

They all went inside of Ethyl, with Nono promptly serving everyone overisized mugs of bonan wine. Lupin eyed the tall glass, and was determined to keep Nono from re-filling it once empty, a hard task. Drinking bonan wine was a good way to keep anxiety away though, Lupin thought. Eka threw an arm around Lupin then. "We have time for this okay? Don't worry." Lupin loved hearing Eka say this, because it always turned out to be true.

Nono eyed Hush for a moment, with its head peering inside and the rest seated outside. "Ipaya?" Nono said, offering the hyroo a mug. Hush gave it a lick, but did not enjoy the taste. Nono laughed, and instead served the hyroo a bowl of mashed bams. "Sou mawani ta dodon oro!"

"Good for the soul and the stomach." Eka translated. "Looks delicious! Enjoy bud!" Already, Hush's face was buried in the bowl, and licked it all up in a second.

"What brings you here?" Lupin asked Nono.

"Nono deliver teaweet from Montore for Balandri Ilk."

"Oh? Renate villagers asked for extra feed?"

"No, no, no. Teaweet is gift." Nono corrected.

"From who? Why?" Lupin asked, perplexed. People in Montore didn't exude generosity, certainly not those with coin.

"Nono know this, but told no say."

Lupin laughed, "that's okay, I think I might know who. Not many endowed philanthropists in Montore." Despite having little evidence, Lupin knew who was responsible for this generous donation. This bit of good news warmed this heart, and the Verido raised a mug-full of bonan wide "to people who care!"

"To people who care!" They all said again, together.

"Do you know if Tarka belongs to anyone?" Lupin asked Nono then.

Nono thought about the name for a moment. "Iana. Abandon, many many year abandon."

Lupin's eyes lit up then, with the Ilk in town they would have all the parts they needed to repair it. Plus they had metal workers they could count on, and a skilled sandfinner to help with the kinks. This was a weight off this Verido's shoulders, Eka could tell. They drank their drinks, and Lupin once again failed to keep Nono from re-filling their cups. The night ended with both eating mashed bams, as a way to soak it all up.
