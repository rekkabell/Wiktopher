The Ilk town of Balandri

— Chapter 13 —

When the group arrived back in Renate, Renzo stopped at the edge of the city, feet over an invisible grave in the sand. This is where the Guard from the Court of Light died. A crowd gathered at the city border, they moved toward the travelers, flowers in hand, instead of stones. They tossed the petals into the air, welcoming their friend back into their lives.

Maka pushed through the crowd, rushing over and pulling Renzo into a hug. The metalworker wept. Lupin had sent the woth ahead with a message, a story of the happenings in Irideri. Maka had begun to cry then already, happy to hear that the lonely apprentice had found peace. Ren laughed. “Not so hard!”

“Hard is all I can do! You know this.” Maka replied, speaking as curtains of liquid cascaded onto the front of Ren's robes.

Lupin flashed Eka a smile. “Aaaaall thanks to Mapoleon the woth!” Eka sniggered when the woth ignored them, continuing to clean the dust off its wings.

"I'm beginning to think it doesn’t want to be named.” Lupin grumbled, "Mapoleon is a great name."

Eka smirked, "woth doesn't agree."

"Ma-ma-ma-poleon?" Lupin tried, the woth did not react, it ignored the Verido and returned to its glass ball. "Not a stutterer," Lupin made a mental note of this.

Maka offered a tent to Eka, Lupin and Hush for their stay here in the city. "You are family now," Maka told them, "to me and everyone in Renate." Indeed, they stayed in town a while, and everyday a different villager would come forward to invite them to tea, to play cards at the tavern, or on excursions in the canyons. Lupin enjoyed the brief moment of stability, of feeling part of a community again. But soon, a voice whispered at their ear, calling on them to keep going. "There is so much more to see out there, come...come!"

And so, Eka and Lupin decided to depart, but first, they had to re-supply. Both went around the village to find food and other items for their trip onward.

“Was this really baked this morning?” asked Eka, inspecting the loaves of bread produced by the local grocer, each one more solid than the next. “This one I could use to hammer down nails."

The grocer continued to knead dough, working up a sweat — perhaps they could knead rock into shapes too. “The Ilk was supposed to have arrived days ago, when it comes we’ll have better stock. In the meantime you’re going to have to make do with what’s left on these shelves. We mix in 50% pebble meal. We need to cut back on teaweet to make sure we can feed the Ilk when it arrives”.

The group had gone through most of the stores Sovereign Kurono had given them, there was little choice but to take what was available here. “If you let it let it rest for 24 hours with ripe bonans. The moisture will help soften the dough," the grocer suggested.

"That's a good tip, thanks!" Eka said.

Another shopkeeper overheard their conversation about the Ilk. “Sometimes Ilk change their route you know, depending on the weather. I just hope it gets here soon. We’re down to bams and kappages, and even THOSE we’re running out of!”

"I thought Renate had loads of fermented kappage always?" Eka said.

"Yes, but none that we can give to transients," one of the shopkeepers said, "yield was bad this year."

Eka nodded. "Understood. Got to feed your own first."

Lupin was busy trying to find directions to the next city on their list, they already had a map but it was outdated and needed corrections. Renate housed a cartographer, working from a tent on the northern side of the camp. The dwelling was easy to find, as one of its faces bore a map of the tent city. Transients would often gather there if in need of help, all of the merchants were listed on it, it was updated regularly. Lupin visited the tent and was glad to find its occupant there, scratching away at a map that spanned the entire room. Lupin looked at it, and saw the writing atop it saying "Edonor". The map was very complex, as this was a big city, rivaling Montore. Individual houses weren't identified, but most shops were there. While looking it over, Lupin spotted Nok's workshop, tucked in between a dye artist and a hatter.

"Nok's shop!" Lupin exclaimed, the sudden sound startling the cartographer.

"Oh dear, have you been standing there long?"

"No no" Lupin said, "You're busy, I'll comeback later."

"Busy, yes. I'm always busy. Since I never stop being busy you ought to ask me what you need now, and it'll be up to me to tell you whether or not I want the job."

"My friend & I are going to Edonor. We need corrections on our map, it's a bit—um, aged. We heard there was a new village between it and Renate."

The cartographer nodded, and then waved a hand, asking for Lupin to come forward. Lupin did just that, approaching slowly, walking over the map and avoiding freshly painted markings. The Verido unrolled the map and laid it over the floor, going down on hands and knees, mirroring the cartographer's position. "I'm Lupin."

"Mapoleon." the cartographer replied.

Lupin's eyes widened then. "Really?" Earlier that morning, this Verido had passed in front of this very tent and had seen the name. Lupin forgot, but the brain inside this head did not, it thought this combination of letters interesting and put it away for later use.

Mapoleon's eyes were stuck on Lupin's map, hand was already busy correcting mistakes, adding newly built bridges, danger areas and such. “Didn’t happen to see an Ilk on your way over here have you? It’s late”

"An Ilk is coming here...?" Lupin was sweating now, "um... whi-which one?"

“Ilk of Balandri” Mapoleon answered. “Supposed to arrive soon, we hope”

Lupin glanced outside through an opening in the wall, heart beating hard in this chest. After the corrections, Lupin thanked the cartographer and hurried back over to Hush. Eka wasn’t back yet and Hush was resting, fast asleep in a recess in the ground — a sure way to keep cool. Lupin waited, with eyes on the horizon. Memories of Volare rushed into this mind, memories of a place this Verido wanted to forget. Just then, Lupin heard people shouting.

“It's here! The Ilk of Balandri is here!”

The Ilk arrived from the south — not its usual path. The whole town was a swirling mess, people amassed empty carts and bags, gathering tools and extra hands, all were getting ready to help unload the Ilk. Lupin noticed a tall ramp, with people running up the steps, getting ready to greet the visitors. This ramp wasn't unfamiliar to Lupin, but these eyes had never seen it from a ground perspective. From down here, the ramp looked frail and unsteady, swaying from side to side as people scaled its numerous steps.

The Ilk arrived at last and came to stand by the platform, Ilk were impressive creatures… skinny, long-legged that end in points, long thin faces with a great big set of horns. On their backs, they carried a small town. “The Ilk of Balandri is here!!” The people of the town shouted. Not long after it arrived, caretakers were already preparing bags of Teaweet grain to attach to its snout. Others were busy brushing the sand of its skin. Other workers pushed steps over to the side of the Ilk, to make a pathway up to the town. People would be coming on and off of it all week.

“Beautiful” Eka said, watching the Ilk’s tower over the tiny city of Renate

.

The quiet town from this morning had turned into a bustling open-air market. Various vendors and store owners from Balandri were setting up shop in a field, previously occupied by tumblewads and Rattats. The place was full of life and colour, people laughing and shouting. Eka took a seat and watched the people, the people of Balandri were tan-skinned and had elongated ears coming out straight from the sides of their heads, like delicate flutes of skin. Most had red colours in their faces, varying in placement and pattern “Hm” she was glancing from Lupin and back to the people of Balandri “Dont you want to say hello Mr. blue?”

Lupin watched them disembark, “To what end?”

Eka took his side, "Suit yourself. They are impressive though aren't they"

“Nearly 400 meters tall”

The Ilk whisperer of Balandri was walking through town, Lupin caught sight of him in the market. They’d never met, but he recognized how he dressed and the wonderfully intricate red patterns on his skin. Lupin had long since discarded his robes, but his blue insignia remained. His family tree printed on flesh, there for all to see but only a handful could decipher it. Klev, the Balandrian, glanced toward him. His eyes locked onto his face, even in a crowd of people the Veridos had a sense of each other. Klev pushed through the market folk and vendors and arrived before him. He placed a hand on Lupin’s heart, and Lupin did the same.

“You are far from home” Klev began to say.

Lupin nodded, “Yes, though technically that would depend entirely on its whereabouts”, only those with a birthplace with legs could say this.

“I admire you! I can’t imagine leaving Balandri. It’s very brave to venture out into the unknown like this, you must have seen much of the world”

“I have” he said, while a confused soup of pride and sadness swished around in his gut, he was happy to have traveled the land and to have met its people. Over time, a hard lump appeared on the side of his head. Whenever thoughts of Volare circulated in his brain the accursed thing latched onto them like a thirsty vampire and gained mass. Then, the simple act of standing on one’s legs became difficult, the weight of it unbearable. “Any word of Volare?” he managed to say, a hand massaging the ghost protrusion on his skull back down.

“Moving along the Ash plains, last I heard.”

“East?” Lupin said, “But that would take them to the Rupture…”

Klev shared his concerns “It’s what I’ve heard. Word is that the Ilk of Volare is seeking death. It’s why I felt I had to come and speak to you when I saw you were of Volare. I don't know if you know this, but their Voice died last year. This may come as a surprise to you, sorry to be the bearer of bad news... but it has to be known. A tragic fall. People are scared, what does this mean for our race? The Ilk may walk into the rupture, and take the city along with it. Balandri and Otora people fear that their Ilk will follow their kin in death”.

The ash plains was a path that the dying would take, the sick, the sad and the elderly. They would walk to the rupture and fall into nothingness, swallowed whole. As far as they knew, the rupture had no bottom and your body would decay in flight. Lupin wondered what or who was causing this 'What are you doing Mago…' He knew she could speak to the Ilk, so why was this happening? Surely she would have found the words to dissuade it from its dark intentions.

Before meeting back with his friends, he went to visit the cartographer once more. Their plans had changed, he needed to have the corrections for the east route on his map. He then spent a good half hour trying to convince the man that he wasn’t going there to die. The concerned cartographer gave him a silver pendant with a sun “The sun is often associated with death in our world, but it is also life,” he said.

Pendant in hand, Lupin returned to their encampment and explained that he needed to travel to the Ash Plains. That evening, Lupin told them about his former life on Volare and how now, he needed to return. He feared for his people. Though there was no way they could there quickly enough, Hush was fast but subject to fatigue. It’s then that Eka mentioned seeing an old Sandfin at the gates of the city.

The next day, they asked around for the captain of the vessel, they were directed to an inn in the center of town. They entered the lobby and Eka walked over to the front desk to ask about the captain’s whereabouts. Lupin waited for her in the lobby, he was lost in thought but awoke from his reveries when he saw a woth land on his shoulder “Ponopo?”

“Eka!” He called out, just as Nono made her way into the Inn, “Eka! You’re not going to believe this.”

It turned out to belong to a friend of Nono, who had fallen ill and abandoned it here years ago, with no intention of keeping it.

Eka saw this as an opportunity, with the Ilk in town they would have all the parts they needed to repair it. Plus they had metal workers they could count on, Ren helped to patch up the holes in the hull. Eka was doing engine work, as usual. While Lupin added fresh paint and purchased some navigation equipment. Soon, the sandfin was repaired and all they needed to do was to test it. On the first run, the rudder fell off, on the second, they found a leak in the fuel tank. The third and fourth run revealed even more problems, but after that they found less and less. On run number 12, the sandfin skidded on the desert plains beautifully, nothing was rattling or threatening to break off. They proceeded to stocking it up with food and supplies for the journey ahead, bags of teaweet flour, crates of wet stones, purple kappages, sweet bams, mapples etc.

Before leaving, Klev, Nono and Ren came by and visited the vessel. All were impressed with their work, Klev was jealous of Lupin for having found strangers that he could share time with. By then, Lupin had explained what had happened and that he was going to help Volare. Klev feared his life, but then again, he feared for his kin. He gifted Lupin and the others with Balandri robes, “They will keep you cool when the sun is high, and will shield your skin from sand.”

They shared an evening of drinks and stories — Klev imparted his best Verido jokes, Ren told some Iride legends; all that, paired with Nono’s sandfin stories, made for quality entertainment.

Later into the night, Klev, Nono and Renzo became red with alcohol. Klev had a {{donmol}}, an old instrument thought to have belonged to Balandri. Klev's fingers stroked the 4 double strings, creating a tune that only other Verido — or wonders like Eka — could interpret. Lupin smiled, attentive to the song, about mountains that could not kneel, moons that could not sleep, and of fires that could not touch. Eka was standing, busy expressing the meaning of the song through absurdly exaggerated gestures. Nono and Ren watched, each mind interpreting the patomime in their own way "Oh, oh, OH! I see, I see! A Durdle, yes. It is hungry! Also, it cannot swim." Nono said. "No, no. It's about the journey of a great warrior," Renzo corrected.

After the song ended, Nono began reciting a poem about a loose bit of twine on Klev's pant leg:

  • Bit of standing twine,
  • Many sands it longs to see,
  • Blade cometh, too soon.

At the phrase, Nono inched forward and cut the bit of twine away with a blade, cackling. Everyone roared with laughter. Nono then proceeded to fill all of the empty mugs with Bam Ale — another Finiku favorite. "Drink up drink up!" Nono said, tapping the bottom of Lupin's cup, causing some of its content to spill. "I am I am!" Lupin cried. All in the group were merry, all, but Nono and Eka, they too had consumed the same amount but it had little effect. “To good genes!" Nono said, raising a mug of ale up high. "I stopped by Ministe during one of my deliveries,” Nono said, turning to Eka. “I asked about your woth,” The Finiku went on, filling up Eka’s mug again.

Experiencing thirst in Nono's presence was impossible, Eka thought, the Finiku was hyperaware, knew the level of liquid present in each mug at all times and insisted that the bottom of the recipient remain unseen.

“We haven’t found its name yet,” Eka said.

“In time, in time!” Nono replied, “I brought my notes to my friend, the woth expert, said that yours is a super woth! They grow to be quite big you know! Used to be more in the old days, it's a rare trait nowadays, one that I thought only the 'tall ones' possessed, if you believe in that sort of thing.” Saying this, Nono's eyes glassed over. "I believe it." Within seconds, Nono drifted to another place entirely, recalling a moment thought lost, something that had happened years ago...

"Where we going?" A young Nono asked, a broad leaf sat over the Finiku's head. Nono's eyes were wet, bulging, body wrapped in a bulky green sweater.

"To the Rupture my love," Winny said, pulling the young sproutling along, "it's your grandmapa's leaping day."

Etyl was sitting atop a Vennec, body wrapped in a thick blanket, head drooping forward, asleep.

Etyl was a loud mouth, strong of will and of body, the person coiled into that blanket was different, tired. Nono was very close to Etyl, it made her very sad when she fell ill. Etyl was the first Finiku to circumnavigate the world by sandfin unaided; and now, she lay in bed, pale and weak. Her illness had robbed her of her independence. After years of this, she sought ways to be free of it, and decided to make her final voyage to the rupture.

Nono and her mother accompanied Etyl there by sandfin. The rupture, as she described, was full with people. Some stood by to discourage people from taking the leap; others were there to try and profit from the dying, in any way they could. Without a word, she watched grandma Ethyl leap into the rupture. Nono saw her disappear into the blackness, swallowed up whole. They stayed for the night, but she couldn’t sleep. Images of her grandmother, falling, kept coming to mind. She snuck out, and went to stand on the edge of the rupture again, as if hoping to see her grandmother emerge, having changed her mind. She wasn’t there, but what she did see…

“...was a giant figure, slipping into the depths! It walked right into the hole.” Nono took a breath, “One of the tall ones!”

Klev had overheard what Nono had said, “I’ve heard rhymes about it, things we’d say when we were kids. One of them goes, ‘One can only be tall if the other is small’.”

Ren too was listening, everyone had gathered around the fire, completely enthralled by this new topic. Lupin and Eka listened, but both kept silent.

The night ended with everyone sleeping around a dying flame, all, but Lupin and Eka. They had been traveling together for some time, yet there was still one thing he had never asked.

“You didn’t look surprised when you saw me come to get you, all those years ago,” Lupin never had this conversation with her, in truth, he’d been avoiding it.

“Someone always comes,” she said with a kind smile.

Eka could tell it was bothering him, “No. Couldn’t have been anyone else. Couldn’t have happened any other way,” she reassured him, as she rolled over to where he was, lying shoulder to shoulder with him. “Though others have come before you, but you weren’t born then so you have a good excuse,” she said with a smirk.

It was foolish, Eka was infinitely kind and ever-mindful of the feelings of others; but the truth was that she had lived many more years than he, he felt like a tiny grain of sand in her presence. Oh how he wished that he too, could live forever.

Eka rolled away again and onto her back, her woth walked along her stomach in search of a good spot to sleep in. When it settled into a fold of her shirt, she began to stroke the ends of the hairs on its back. A low hum sounded, escaping from her throat. She hummed for a while, until she knew for certain that Lupin was asleep.
